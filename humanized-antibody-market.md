<p><p>The humanized antibody market comprises primarily two types: chimeric antibodies and modified antibodies. Chimeric antibodies combine mouse and human elements to enhance efficacy while reducing immunogenicity, making them effective in targeting specific diseases. Modified antibodies undergo engineering to improve their properties, such as stability, affinity, or half-life, thereby increasing their therapeutic potential. Both types play crucial roles in the development of targeted therapies for various conditions, particularly cancer and autoimmune diseases, contributing to advancements in personalized medicine.</p></p>
