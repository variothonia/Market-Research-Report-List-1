<p><p>The automotive elastomer market includes several types, each serving specific applications. Natural rubber offers excellent elasticity and resilience, while Styrene-Butadiene Rubber (SBR) is valued for its abrasion resistance. Nitrile elastomers are used in fuel and oil-resistant applications. EPDM provides weather resistance and durability, making it ideal for sealing and insulation. Silicone rubber offers high-temperature stability, and fluoroelastomers are known for their chemical resistance. Styrene block copolymers combine rubber-like properties with thermoplastic processing. Other elastomers cater to niche requirements in automotive applications.</p></p>
