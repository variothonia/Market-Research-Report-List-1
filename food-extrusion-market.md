<p><p>The food extrusion market consists of two primary types: cold extrusion and hot extrusion. Cold extrusion involves shaping food products at room temperature, often used for making pasta or snacks, preserving delicate ingredients and textures. In contrast, hot extrusion uses heat to cook and shape food during processing, suitable for products like cereals and pet food, enhancing digestibility and texture. Both methods appeal to various segments in the food industry, offering unique benefits based on product requirements and consumer preferences.</p></p>
